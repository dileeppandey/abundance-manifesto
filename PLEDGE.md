# The Abundance Pledge

*A personal commitment to the principles of the Abundance Manifesto.*

---

## The Pledge

I have read the [Abundance Manifesto](MANIFESTO.md).

I believe that scarcity, in the 21st century, is largely a policy choice — enforced through intellectual property law, planned obsolescence, and platform monopolies — not a natural condition.

I commit, in my work and my community, to:

1. **Choose openness** — I will prefer open-source, open-hardware, and open-knowledge tools. Where I create, I will share my work under licenses that prevent re-enclosure.

2. **Build locally, share globally** — I will support distributed manufacturing, local production networks, and the infrastructure of making in my community.

3. **Design for regeneration** — I will refuse waste, prioritize repairability, and consider the ecological impact of what I build and consume.

4. **Support cooperative governance** — I will favor platforms, services, and institutions owned and governed by their users and workers. I will not build extractive platforms.

5. **Share access, not just ownership** — I will contribute to libraries of tools, shared resources, and commons infrastructure. I will measure wealth by what I can use with others, not what I hold alone.

I do not pledge perfection. I pledge direction.

---

## Why Sign?

Because movements need witnesses. Because saying something publicly changes how you act. Because a growing list of names is itself a form of evidence — that this is not a fringe idea, but a converging consensus.

Your name here is a small token of abundance: a contribution to a commons that compounds.

## How to Sign

Signing is done by opening a Pull Request to add your name to [SIGNATORIES.md](SIGNATORIES.md). This makes your signature permanent, verifiable, and part of the project's history.

### Step-by-step

1. **Fork this repository** — click "Fork" in the top right of the GitHub page

2. **Edit `SIGNATORIES.md`** — add a row to the table with your name, location (optional), domain/field, and today's date

   ```
   | Your Name | City, Country (optional) | Your field | YYYY-MM-DD |
   ```

3. **Open a Pull Request** — title it `Sign pledge: [Your Name]`

4. **That's it.** Once merged, your signature is part of the public record.

### Prefer anonymity?

Use a handle or pseudonym. Location is optional. What matters is the commitment, not the identity.

### Not on GitHub?

Open an [Issue](../../issues/new) titled "Sign pledge: [Your Name or handle]" and a maintainer will add your name.
