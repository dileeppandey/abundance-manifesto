# Open Alternatives: Communication

*Messaging, email, video, and social networks — the infrastructure of human coordination.*

The platforms we communicate through shape how we organize, what we see, and who profits from our attention. This category maps open alternatives to the surveillance-ad platforms that dominate communication today.

---

## Messaging

### [Signal](https://signal.org)
**Replaces:** WhatsApp, iMessage, SMS  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

End-to-end encrypted messaging and calls. Signal Protocol is open source and has become the gold standard for secure messaging — WhatsApp itself uses it. Run by a nonprofit foundation; no ads, no data harvesting. The closest thing to private communication at consumer scale.

**Get involved:** [Contribute](https://github.com/signalapp) · [Donate](https://signal.org/donate/)

---

### [Matrix / Element](https://matrix.org)
**Replaces:** Slack, Microsoft Teams, Discord  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

An open, federated protocol for real-time communication. Unlike Slack or Teams, no single company owns the network — anyone can run a server, and servers interoperate. Element is the flagship client. Used by the German government, the French military, and thousands of open-source communities.

**Get involved:** [Contribute to Matrix](https://github.com/matrix-org) · [Self-host a server](https://matrix.org/docs/guides/installing-synapse)

---

### [Briar](https://briarproject.org)
**Replaces:** WhatsApp (in high-risk or offline contexts)  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Peer-to-peer encrypted messaging that works over Tor, WiFi, or Bluetooth — no central server required. Designed for activists, journalists, and communities where internet access is unreliable or surveilled.

**Get involved:** [Contribute](https://code.briarproject.org/briar/briar)

## Email

### [Thunderbird](https://www.thunderbird.net)
**Replaces:** Outlook, Apple Mail  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Open-source email client maintained by Mozilla. Full-featured, cross-platform, no lock-in. Supports encryption via OpenPGP natively.

**Get involved:** [Contribute](https://github.com/mozilla/releases-comm-central) · [Donate](https://give.thunderbird.net/)

---

### [Proton Mail](https://proton.me/mail)
**Replaces:** Gmail, Outlook  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

End-to-end encrypted email based in Switzerland. Open-source apps and encryption libraries. No ad-based business model. Proton also offers calendar, drive, and VPN under the same model.

**Get involved:** [Contribute](https://github.com/ProtonMail)

---

### [Tutanota](https://tuta.com)
**Replaces:** Gmail  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Encrypted email with a fully open-source client. Powered by renewable energy. Based in Germany.

**Get involved:** [Contribute](https://github.com/tutao/tutanota)

## Video & Calls

### [Jitsi Meet](https://meet.jit.si)
**Replaces:** Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Free, open-source video conferencing. No account needed. Can be self-hosted — many organizations run their own instance. Used by universities, NGOs, and governments globally.

**Get involved:** [Contribute](https://github.com/jitsi/jitsi-meet) · [Self-host](https://jitsi.github.io/handbook/docs/devops-guide/)

---

### [BigBlueButton](https://bigbluebutton.org)
**Replaces:** Zoom (for education and webinars)  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Open-source video conferencing designed specifically for education. Includes virtual whiteboards, breakout rooms, polling, and shared notes. Self-hostable. Widely adopted by universities.

**Get involved:** [Contribute](https://github.com/bigbluebutton/bigbluebutton)

## Social Networks

### [Mastodon](https://joinmastodon.org)
**Replaces:** Twitter / X  
**Principle:** [IV] Cooperative Stewardship  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Federated, open-source social network. No central authority — thousands of independent servers (instances) interoperate through the ActivityPub protocol. No algorithmic manipulation, no ads, no surveillance capitalism. Instances are governed by their own communities.

**Get involved:** [Pick an instance](https://joinmastodon.org/servers) · [Contribute](https://github.com/mastodon/mastodon)

---

### [Pixelfed](https://pixelfed.org)
**Replaces:** Instagram  
**Principle:** [IV] Cooperative Stewardship  
**Maturity:** Maturing  

Federated photo-sharing platform. Part of the ActivityPub "Fediverse" — interoperates with Mastodon. No ads, no algorithmic feed, no data harvesting.

**Get involved:** [Join an instance](https://pixelfed.org/servers) · [Contribute](https://github.com/pixelfed/pixelfed)

---

### [PeerTube](https://joinpeertube.org)
**Replaces:** YouTube  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Maturing  

Federated video hosting using peer-to-peer technology to reduce hosting costs. No ads, no algorithmic recommendations, no surveillance. Instances are run by communities, universities, and media organizations.

**Get involved:** [Find an instance](https://joinpeertube.org/instances) · [Contribute](https://github.com/Chocobozzz/PeerTube)

---

### [Diaspora](https://diasporafoundation.org)
**Replaces:** Facebook  
**Principle:** [IV] Cooperative Stewardship  
**Maturity:** Maturing  

Decentralized social network where users own their data. No central server, no ads, no corporate ownership. One of the earliest federated social networks.

**Get involved:** [Join a pod](https://diasporafoundation.org/#get-started) · [Contribute](https://github.com/diaspora/diaspora)
