# Open Alternatives: Energy

*Community power generation, open grid tools, and energy monitoring.*

Energy is the precondition for everything else. A world with abundant clean energy is a world with abundant food, clean water, manufacturing capacity, and computation. This category maps the tools and models for democratizing energy — moving from monopoly utilities to community-owned, distributed generation.

---

## Community Energy Models

### [Energy Communities (EU Framework)](https://energy-communities.eu)
**Replaces:** Monopoly utility companies  
**Principle:** [IV] Cooperative Stewardship  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

The European Union's legal framework for "energy communities" — groups of citizens, small businesses, and local authorities that collectively own and manage renewable energy assets. Over 9,000 energy communities operate across Europe. Members generate, share, and sell power locally, bypassing or complementing the traditional utility model.

**Get involved:** [Find or start an energy community](https://energy-communities.eu/start/) · [EU policy overview](https://energy.ec.europa.eu/topics/markets-and-consumers/energy-communities_en)

---

### [Rocky Mountain Institute (RMI)](https://rmi.org)
**Replaces:** Fossil fuel consulting and planning  
**Principle:** [III] Regenerative Circularity  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Nonprofit organization developing open-source tools, models, and frameworks for the clean energy transition. Publishes free resources for community energy planning, grid decarbonization, and building efficiency. Their open-source models have influenced energy policy in dozens of countries.

**Get involved:** [Free resources](https://rmi.org/insights/) · [Open data tools](https://rmi.org/our-work/)

## Open-Source Energy Software

### [OpenEnergyMonitor](https://openenergymonitor.org)
**Replaces:** Proprietary smart meters and energy monitors  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Open-source hardware and software for energy monitoring — electricity consumption, solar generation, heat pump performance. Build your own monitor from open designs or buy assembled hardware. All data stays local. GPL licensed.

**Get involved:** [Contribute](https://github.com/openenergymonitor) · [Community forum](https://community.openenergymonitor.org)

---

### [Home Assistant Energy](https://www.home-assistant.io/docs/energy/)
**Replaces:** Proprietary energy management platforms  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Open-source home automation platform with built-in energy management — tracks solar production, battery storage, grid consumption, and electric vehicle charging. Fully local: no cloud dependency, no subscription, no data harvesting. 500,000+ active installs.

**Get involved:** [Contribute](https://github.com/home-assistant/core) · [Documentation](https://www.home-assistant.io)

---

### [Gridlabd](https://www.gridlabd.org)
**Replaces:** Proprietary grid simulation software  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Open-source power grid simulation and analysis tool developed by the US Department of Energy. Used by utilities, researchers, and energy planners to model distributed energy resources, microgrids, and grid transitions. BSD licensed.

**Get involved:** [Contribute](https://github.com/slacgismo/gridlabd)

---

### [PyPSA (Python for Power System Analysis)](https://pypsa.org)
**Replaces:** Proprietary energy system optimization tools  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Open-source Python toolbox for simulating and optimizing modern power systems — renewable energy integration, sector coupling (power + heating + transport), grid expansion planning. MIT licensed. Used by research institutions and grid operators globally.

**Get involved:** [Contribute](https://github.com/PyPSA/PyPSA)

## Open Hardware for Energy

### [OpenSolarMap](https://opensolarmap.org)
**Replaces:** Proprietary solar potential assessment services  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Maturing  

Open-source tool for mapping solar potential using publicly available data. Helps communities and individuals assess the feasibility of solar installations without paying for proprietary consulting.

---

### [LibreSolar](https://libre.solar)
**Replaces:** Proprietary solar charge controllers and battery management systems  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Maturing  

Open-source hardware designs for solar energy storage systems — MPPT charge controllers, battery management, DC/DC converters. All designs released under Creative Commons and CERN OHL. Designed for off-grid and community energy applications in resource-constrained settings.

**Get involved:** [Contribute designs](https://github.com/LibreSolar) · [Community](https://community.libre.solar)
