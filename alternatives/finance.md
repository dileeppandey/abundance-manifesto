# Open Alternatives: Finance

*Banking, payments, investment, and capital allocation.*

Finance is the nervous system of the economy. Who controls the flow of capital determines what gets built. Proprietary finance — commercial banks, venture capital, payment monopolies — allocates resources toward profit maximization. This category maps alternatives that align capital allocation with community benefit.

---

## Cooperative Banking

### [Credit Unions](https://www.woccu.org)
**Replaces:** Commercial banks  
**Principle:** [IV] Cooperative Stewardship  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Credit unions are member-owned financial cooperatives. Profits return to members as lower loan rates, higher savings rates, and lower fees — not to shareholders. Over 86,000 credit unions serve 375 million members globally. In the US, credit unions are exempt from federal income tax because they serve their members, not profit-seeking owners.

**Get involved:** [Find a credit union](https://www.mycreditunion.gov/about-credit-unions/credit-union-locator) · [Start a credit union](https://www.ncua.gov/regulation-supervision/chartering-and-field-membership)

### [Triodos Bank](https://www.triodos.com)
**Replaces:** Commercial banks for ethical investment  
**Principle:** [IV] Cooperative Stewardship  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

European bank that publishes every organization it lends to. Only finances businesses and projects that benefit people and the planet: renewable energy, organic farming, affordable housing, arts, education. Not extractive finance. A working model of transparent, mission-driven banking.

## Community Finance

### [Kiva](https://www.kiva.org)
**Replaces:** Predatory lending and microfinance  
**Principle:** [IV] Cooperative Stewardship  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Platform enabling individuals to lend as little as $25 to entrepreneurs globally. Zero-interest loans to small businesses, farmers, and individuals who lack access to traditional banking. 98% repayment rate. Has facilitated over $2 billion in loans to 4+ million borrowers.

**Get involved:** [Lend](https://www.kiva.org/lend) · [Volunteer](https://www.kiva.org/volunteer)

---

### [Open Collective](https://opencollective.com)
**Replaces:** Proprietary fiscal sponsorship and donation platforms  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Open-source platform for transparent collective finances. Communities, open-source projects, and mutual aid groups can receive and spend money with full financial transparency — every transaction is public. No hidden overhead. Used by thousands of open-source projects including Vue.js, Webpack, and many others.

**Get involved:** [Start a collective](https://opencollective.com/create) · [Contribute](https://github.com/opencollective/opencollective)

## Open Payments Infrastructure

### [Stripe (partial)](https://stripe.com) + [Open Source Alternatives]
**Note:** Stripe is proprietary. For self-hosted, open-source payment processing:

### [Kill Bill](https://killbill.io)
**Replaces:** Stripe, Recurly, Chargebee  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Open-source subscription billing and payment platform. Apache 2.0 licensed. Full billing, invoicing, and payment orchestration. Self-hostable, meaning no per-transaction fees to a third party.

**Get involved:** [Contribute](https://github.com/killbill/killbill)

---

### [GNU Taler](https://taler.net)
**Replaces:** PayPal, Stripe (for privacy-preserving payments)  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Maturing  

Privacy-preserving digital payment system developed by GNU. Payments are anonymous for buyers but taxable — designed to be compatible with existing legal frameworks while protecting consumer privacy. Deployed by several European institutions.

**Get involved:** [Contribute](https://git.taler.net) · [Documentation](https://docs.taler.net)

## Open Source Finance Tools

### [GnuCash](https://www.gnucash.org)
**Replaces:** QuickBooks, FreshBooks  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Open-source accounting software for individuals and small businesses. Double-entry bookkeeping, invoicing, reports. GPL licensed. Used by small businesses and nonprofits globally.

**Get involved:** [Contribute](https://wiki.gnucash.org/wiki/Development)

### [Firefly III](https://www.firefly-iii.org)

**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness

Self-hosted personal finance manager. Tracks income, expenses, budgets, and goals. Full data ownership — no third party sees your financial data. AGPL licensed.

## Impact Investment & Grant Infrastructure

### [Gitcoin](https://www.gitcoin.co)
**Replaces:** Venture capital for open-source projects  
**Principle:** [IV] Cooperative Stewardship  
**Maturity:** Maturing  

Platform for funding open-source development through quadratic funding — a mechanism where small donations from many people are matched by a larger pool, amplifying grassroots community support over large donors. Has distributed over $50 million to open-source projects.

**Get involved:** [Fund a project](https://www.gitcoin.co/grants) · [Contribute](https://github.com/gitcoinco)

---

### [Open Collective Foundation](https://opencollective.foundation)
**Replaces:** Traditional nonprofit fiscal sponsorship  
**Principle:** [IV] Cooperative Stewardship  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

501(c)(3) fiscal sponsor for open-source projects, mutual aid groups, and community organizations. Provides legal nonprofit status without requiring groups to incorporate separately. All finances are transparent and public.

**Get involved:** [Apply for fiscal hosting](https://opencollective.foundation/apply)
