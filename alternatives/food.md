# Open Alternatives: Food & Agriculture

*Seeds, farming tools, distribution networks, and knowledge commons.*

Food is the most fundamental form of abundance. Yet our food system is increasingly enclosed: seed patents restrict what farmers can grow, proprietary data platforms extract value from agricultural knowledge, and corporate supply chains concentrate control over what gets produced and who gets fed.

This category maps the tools and movements that keep food knowledge open, seeds in the commons, and farming power in farmers' hands.

---

## Seed Commons

### [Open Source Seed Initiative (OSSI)](https://osseeds.org)
**Replaces:** Patented seeds (Monsanto/Bayer, Syngenta, Corteva)  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

OSSI has "pledged" over 500 crop varieties — a commitment that these seeds will always be free to save, share, breed from, and sell. Not a legal license but a moral covenant: anyone who receives an OSSI pledged seed must pass those freedoms on. Modeled explicitly on the open-source software copyleft concept applied to biology.

**Get involved:** [Sign the pledge](https://osseeds.org/about/the-pledge/) · [Buy pledged seeds](https://osseeds.org/seeds/)

---

### [Navdanya](https://navdanya.org)
**Replaces:** Commercial seed suppliers  
**Principle:** [IV] Cooperative Stewardship  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Founded by Vandana Shiva, Navdanya is an Indian network of seed keepers and organic farmers. Has conserved over 5,000 varieties of rice, wheat, and other crops. Operates community seed banks across India where farmers freely deposit and withdraw traditional varieties.

**Get involved:** [Support seed saving](https://navdanya.org/get-involved) · [Seed banks](https://navdanya.org/seed-freedom)

---

### [Seed Savers Exchange](https://www.seedsavers.org)
**Replaces:** Commercial seed industry  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

US nonprofit maintaining a living collection of over 20,000 heirloom seed varieties contributed by thousands of members. All varieties are available for members to grow and save. Preserves agricultural genetic diversity against the narrowing of commercial seed offerings.

**Get involved:** [Become a member](https://www.seedsavers.org/membership) · [Donate seeds](https://www.seedsavers.org/donate-seeds)

## Open Agricultural Knowledge

### [OpenFarm](https://openfarm.cc)
**Replaces:** Proprietary agronomy databases  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Community-built database of crop growing guides. Free, open-source, CC-licensed. Anyone can contribute growing information for any crop. Used by home gardeners, urban farmers, and open agricultural software like FarmHack and FarmBot.

**Get involved:** [Contribute growing guides](https://openfarm.cc/en/crops/new) · [Contribute code](https://github.com/openfarmcc/OpenFarm)

---

### [CGIAR Open Access Repository](https://cgiar.org/research/open-access/)
**Replaces:** Paywalled agricultural research  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

The Consultative Group for International Agricultural Research (CGIAR) has committed to open access for all its research outputs — breeding lines, trait data, genomic data, and publications. The most significant publicly-funded agricultural research institution in the world. Freely accessible to any farmer, researcher, or program globally.

**Get involved:** [Access the repository](https://repository.cgiar.org)

## Open Farming Hardware

### [FarmBot](https://farm.bot)
**Replaces:** Proprietary precision agriculture systems  
**Principle:** [II] Design Global, Make Local  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Open-source, automated, precision farming machine for garden-scale food production. Software is fully open source (MIT license). Hardware designs are open source. Can be self-built or purchased assembled. Controlled via a web interface; integrates with OpenFarm's crop database.

**Get involved:** [Contribute to software](https://github.com/FarmBot) · [Build your own](https://genesis.farm.bot/docs/intro)

---

### [FarmHack](https://farmhack.org)
**Replaces:** Proprietary farm equipment  
**Principle:** [II] Design Global, Make Local  
**Maturity:** Maturing  

Open-source community of farmers building and sharing DIY farm tools. A library of tools designed, built, and documented by working farmers — covers everything from soil blockers to cover crop rollers to irrigation timers. All under Creative Commons.

**Get involved:** [Browse tools](https://farmhack.org/tools) · [Contribute a tool](https://farmhack.org/forums)

## Alternative Distribution Networks

### [Food Assembly (La Ruche qui dit Oui)](https://thefoodassembly.com)
**Replaces:** Industrial food supply chains and supermarkets  
**Principle:** [IV] Cooperative Stewardship  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Platform connecting local farmers directly to consumer groups (food assemblies). Farmers keep 80% of revenue (vs. ~20-30% in supermarket models). No food waste — orders placed before harvest. Platform is partially open source. Operating in France, UK, Germany, and other EU countries.

---

### [Solid Ground (formerly Second Harvest)](https://www.solid-ground.org)
**Replaces:** Corporate food distribution  
**Principle:** [IV] Cooperative Stewardship  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Community food banking and distribution network model. Rescues surplus food from producers and retailers and distributes it to people facing food insecurity. Open operational model that community organizations can replicate.

---

### [Community Supported Agriculture (CSA)](https://www.nal.usda.gov/legacy/afsic/community-supported-agriculture)
**Replaces:** Retail food supply chains  
**Principle:** [V] Access Over Ownership  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

A model, not a product: members of a community pre-pay for a season's share of a farm's harvest. Risk is shared between farmer and consumer. Farmers get upfront capital; members get fresh, local food. Tens of thousands of CSA farms operate globally. A direct implementation of Principle 5 applied to food.

**Get involved:** [Find a CSA](https://www.localharvest.org/csa/)
