# Open Alternatives: Manufacturing

*Open hardware, distributed fabrication, and alternatives to proprietary supply chains.*

This category is the physical heart of the manifesto. Design Global, Manufacture Local means that the blueprints for the machines we need to live — tractors, medical devices, housing components, energy systems — should be freely shared, locally buildable, and cooperatively governed.

---

## Open Hardware Platforms

### [Open Source Ecology (OSE)](https://www.opensourceecology.org)
**Replaces:** Proprietary agricultural and construction equipment  
**Principle:** [II] Design Global, Make Local  
**Maturity:** Maturing  

OSE's **Global Village Construction Set (GVCS)** is an open-source collection of 50 industrial machines — tractors, brick presses, CNC torch tables, circuit mills, hydrogen generators — needed to build a sustainable civilization. All designs are freely available. Machines are designed to share modular components (the "Power Cube" powers both a tractor and a sawmill).

The project's honest self-assessment: quality control in distributed builds remains a challenge. The GVCS is a proof of concept and a platform for development, not a finished product. But it is the most ambitious implementation of the DGML principle in existence.

**Get involved:** [Contribute to designs](https://wiki.opensourceecology.org) · [Support OSE](https://www.opensourceecology.org/donate/)

---

### [OSHWA (Open Source Hardware Association)](https://www.oshwa.org)
**Replaces:** Proprietary hardware certification bodies  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

The certification body and standards organization for open-source hardware. Maintains the Open Source Hardware Definition and a directory of certified open-source hardware projects. The CERN Open Hardware Licence (OHL) — a copyleft license for hardware — was developed in collaboration with OSHWA.

**Get involved:** [Certify your hardware](https://certification.oshwa.org) · [Contribute to standards](https://github.com/oshwa)

## Fabrication Tools

### [RepRap Project](https://reprap.org)
**Replaces:** Proprietary 3D printers  
**Principle:** [II] Design Global, Make Local  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

The original self-replicating open-source 3D printer. RepRap printers can print many of their own parts, enabling distributed manufacturing of the manufacturing tools themselves. The RepRap project gave birth to the entire consumer 3D printer industry — virtually every affordable 3D printer today descends from RepRap designs.

**Get involved:** [Community wiki](https://reprap.org/wiki/Main_Page) · [Forum](https://forum.reprap.org)

---

### [Prusa Research](https://www.prusa3d.com)
**Replaces:** Proprietary 3D printers (MakerBot, Stratasys)  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Czech company making open-source 3D printers. All designs are released under GPL. Prusa has become the standard for reliable, prosumer-grade FDM printing while maintaining its open-source commitment. During COVID-19, Prusa immediately open-sourced face shield designs and coordinated global production.

**Get involved:** [Contribute designs](https://www.printables.com) · [Buy hardware to fund development](https://www.prusa3d.com)

---

### [FreeCAD](https://www.freecad.org)
**Replaces:** Autodesk Fusion 360, SolidWorks  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Maturing  

Open-source parametric 3D CAD modeler. Fully LGPL licensed. The most capable open-source option for mechanical engineering design. Maturing rapidly — version 1.0 (2024) marked a significant stability milestone.

**Get involved:** [Contribute](https://github.com/FreeCAD/FreeCAD) · [Documentation](https://wiki.freecad.org)

---

### [KiCad](https://www.kicad.org)
**Replaces:** Altium Designer, Eagle (now Autodesk)  
**Principle:** [I] Radical Openness  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Open-source electronics design automation (EDA) suite — schematic capture and PCB layout. GPL licensed. Used by CERN, SpaceX, and thousands of open hardware projects. The most capable open-source EDA tool available.

**Get involved:** [Contribute](https://www.kicad.org/contribute/) · [Forum](https://forum.kicad.info)

## FabLab Networks

### [FabLab Network](https://fabfoundation.org)
**Replaces:** Proprietary manufacturing services  
**Principle:** [II] Design Global, Make Local  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

A global network of 2,500+ community fabrication labs in 100+ countries, sharing open designs and standards. Any FabLab with a basic set of tools (laser cutter, CNC router, 3D printer, vinyl cutter) can produce anything in the shared design library. Founded by MIT's Center for Bits and Atoms.

**Get involved:** [Find a FabLab](https://www.fablabs.io/labs) · [Start a FabLab](https://fabfoundation.org/how-to-start-a-fablab/)

---

### [Precious Plastic](https://www.preciousplastic.com)
**Replaces:** Industrial plastic recycling facilities  
**Principle:** [III] Regenerative Circularity  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

Open-source machines for recycling plastic at a community scale — shredder, extruder, injection molder, and sheet press. All designs are free to download and build. A global network of workspaces collects and transforms plastic waste into new products locally.

**Get involved:** [Download machines](https://www.preciousplastic.com/solutions/machines) · [Join the community](https://community.preciousplastic.com)

## Open Medical Devices

### [Open Source Medical Supplies (OSMS)](https://opensourcemedicalsupplies.org)
**Replaces:** Proprietary medical supply chains  
**Principle:** [II] Design Global, Make Local  
**Maturity:** Maturing  

Directory and coordination hub for open-source medical device designs — PPE, ventilators, diagnostics, prosthetics. Emerged from the COVID-19 crisis when centralized supply chains failed. Connects designers, makers, and healthcare workers globally.

**Get involved:** [Contribute designs](https://opensourcemedicalsupplies.org/contribute/)

---

### [e-NABLE Community](https://enablingthefuture.org)
**Replaces:** Proprietary prosthetic manufacturers  
**Principle:** [II] Design Global, Make Local  
**Maturity:** Production-ready  

A global network of volunteers 3D printing free prosthetic hands and arms for people in need. Open-source designs, local production. Has delivered over 10,000 devices in 50+ countries at a fraction of the cost of commercial prosthetics.

**Get involved:** [Join as a maker](https://enablingthefuture.org/get-involved/)
